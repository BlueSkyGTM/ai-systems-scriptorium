# Incident Response Policy (IRP-330)

## Section 1 — Definition

A security incident is any event that compromises the confidentiality, integrity, or
availability of customer data. A suspected breach is treated as an incident until proven
otherwise.

## Section 2 — Reporting timeline

A confirmed personal-data breach must be reported to the supervisory authority within
seventy-two hours of the firm becoming aware of it. If the breach is likely to result in
high risk to affected customers, those customers must also be notified without undue
delay.

## Section 3 — Containment

On detection, the on-call responder isolates the affected system, preserves logs for
forensic review, and opens an incident record. No affected system may be wiped or rebuilt
until the forensic snapshot is captured.

## Section 4 — Post-incident review

Within ten business days of closing an incident, the team produces a post-incident review
naming the root cause, the timeline, and the corrective actions. Corrective actions are
tracked to completion and verified by the security officer.
