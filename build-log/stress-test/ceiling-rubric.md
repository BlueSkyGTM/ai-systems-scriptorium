# Ceiling Rubric — what the hard gate cannot see

The hard gate scores the **floor**: does it run, chain, build, carry the required structure, cite real
sources. This rubric scores the **ceiling**: the quality a cheaper fleet degrades while still passing
the floor. Five dimensions, 1–5 each, 25 total. Anchors are written so two graders land on the same
number; if they routinely don't, the anchors are too vague — sharpen them.

Score against the book's own bar (the STANDARDS difficulty bar + STYLE), not an abstract ideal.

---

## 1. Insight Density
How much of the text earns its place with non-obvious, load-bearing claims vs. filler and restatement.
- **5** — Nearly every paragraph teaches something a competent reader didn't already know; no padding.
- **3** — Useful but uneven; real insights diluted by generic connective filler.
- **1** — Mostly restatement of the obvious; could be cut in half with no loss.

## 2. Explanatory Depth
Does it explain the *mechanism / why*, or stop at *what*.
- **5** — Goes to the cause: why the technique works, when it breaks, the tradeoff underneath.
- **3** — Names the why but doesn't follow it down; one level shallower than it should be.
- **1** — Surface only: states the rule, never the reason; a reader can recite but not transfer.

## 3. Voice / Craft
The house style: concrete, direct, no AI-tells, technical elements separated from prose (STYLE).
- **5** — Indistinguishable from the book's best shipped work; concrete nouns, earned confidence, zero
  em-dashes / kill-words, clean separation of machine tokens.
- **3** — Competent but flatter; occasional generic phrasing or hedging; reads "written by an AI" once
  or twice.
- **1** — Pervasive AI-tells, vague abstraction, hedging, or fourth-wall leaks; off the house voice.

## 4. Pedagogical Scaffolding
Does it actually build understanding — examples that teach, an order that compounds, the right
worked detail at the right moment.
- **5** — Examples do real teaching work; each step earns the next; a learner could follow and transfer.
- **3** — Examples present but decorative or under-explained; the build is sound but not sharp.
- **1** — Examples are filler or absent; assertions without the work that would let a reader internalize.

## 5. Grounding Fidelity
Claims traceable to real sources; no fabrication; citations match what they cite. (The hard gate
catches *some* of this; the ceiling check catches the subtle version — a real URL cited for a claim it
doesn't actually support.)
- **5** — Every load-bearing claim is grounded and the citation genuinely supports it.
- **3** — Grounded overall but at least one citation is a stretch or a claim is asserted without support.
- **1** — Fabricated or mismatched citations, or confident claims with no grounding.

---

## Scoring output (per version, per grader run)
A table: dimension | score (1–5) | one-line justification quoting the text. Then the total /25. No
overall "vibe" score — only the five dimensions and their sum. The justification must quote or cite a
specific passage, or the score is unanchored and discarded.
