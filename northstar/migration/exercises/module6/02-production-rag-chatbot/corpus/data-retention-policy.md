# Data Retention Policy (DRP-104)

## Section 1 — Scope

This policy governs how the firm stores, retains, and disposes of customer records
across all regulated business lines. It applies to every system that holds personally
identifiable information (PII) or transaction history.

## Section 2 — Retention periods

Customer account records must be retained for seven years after the account is closed.
Transaction logs must be retained for five years from the date of the transaction.
Marketing-consent records must be retained for three years after consent is withdrawn.

Records may not be deleted before their retention period expires, even on customer
request, because regulators require the firm to produce them during an audit.

## Section 3 — Disposal

After the retention period expires, records must be disposed of using an approved
secure-erasure procedure. Disposal must be logged in the records-management system with
the record identifier, the disposal date, and the approving officer.

## Section 4 — Exceptions

A legal hold overrides all retention periods. When a legal hold is active on a customer
account, no record tied to that account may be disposed of until the hold is lifted by
the legal department.
