## Example Entry (Question: "Tell me about a time you led a technical initiative without formal authority.")

**Stated signal:** Ownership (secondary: Communication/Influence)

**Latent signal:** The question is not asking whether you can lead without a title. It is asking whether you can operate in an organization where influence is the mechanism and authority is not available. A weak answer demonstrates personal technical work; the real probe is whether you changed behavior in other people without being able to order them to change.

**Frontier lab context:** At a frontier lab, the emphasis shifts to whether the initiative touched safety or alignment in any way, and whether you handled the lack of authority by building consensus or by finding the technical argument that made the answer obvious. The values-alignment signal is weighted here; showing that you brought others along by making the reasoning transparent matters more than showing that you shipped fast.

**Enterprise context:** At an enterprise, the emphasis shifts to stakeholder management, compliance boundaries, and whether you documented the initiative in a way that survived your departure. The question is whether you can operate inside a governance structure without formal rank. Showing that you looped in legal, security, or a compliance team -- and that the initiative survived a review -- is the signal that lands.
