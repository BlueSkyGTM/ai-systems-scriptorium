# Module 3: Behavioral Interviews

You have the Algorithm and you have run it deep. What you do not have yet is reflex. Reflex comes from
reps, and this module is the first bank of them: the four steps applied to real behavioral questions,
over and over, until the moves stop being effort.

This is also the module where the book's method earns its keep. Behavioral rounds are where strong
engineers lose offers they should win, because they walk in with a list of stories and no way to aim
them. You will walk in with something better: a method for building the right story toward the right
signal, and a bank of your own answers already stress-tested.

## How to Read a Worked Example

Every example in this module runs the full Algorithm: the question, the decomposition that names the
signal, the construction that builds toward it, the stress-test that makes it unfakeable. Read each one
for the move, not the words.

This matters because the failure mode of behavioral prep is memorization. A candidate who memorizes a
model answer can deliver it once, fluently, to the exact question it was written for, and falls apart
the moment the question arrives in a form they did not rehearse. The worked examples here are not scripts
to recite. They are demonstrations of the reasoning, run on real questions, so you can run the same
reasoning on your own experience. The story is always yours; the method is what transfers.

## The Four Categories

Behavioral questions cluster into four categories, each collecting a different signal. One lesson each:

1. **Ownership Stories** test whether you drove an outcome or watched one happen. The discipline is the
   first person: "I decided," not "we explored."
2. **Conflict Stories** test whether you can disagree, hold a position, and still move with the team.
   The pattern is disagree-and-commit, with escalation proportional to the stakes.
3. **Failure Stories** test calibration: can you name a real failure, own it, and show what changed?
   The Learning step carries the signal here.
4. **Influence Stories** test whether you move people without authority, and at the staff level, whether
   your work changed what others could build.

## What This Module Feeds Forward

By the end you have a behavioral bank in your prep dossier: your own stories, one or more per category,
each run through the full Algorithm and the weak-answer audit from Module 2. That bank is the artifact
you draw from in every behavioral round, and the technical and systems-design banks ahead are built the
same way. The reps start here.
