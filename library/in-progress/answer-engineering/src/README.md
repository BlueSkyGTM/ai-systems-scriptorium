# Introduction

You built the thing. A terminal coding agent that ships real diffs, a governed fleet that runs under
a budget, an exam system that grades itself. The other books in this library taught you to build AI
systems that hold under production load. This book teaches the last skill the job actually screens for:
explaining what you built to someone who did not watch you build it, under time pressure, in a room
where the offer is decided.

That skill is not the same as the building. Strong engineers lose offers every week because the
interview is a different game with different rules, and nobody taught them the rules. They reach for
memorized answers, the stock STAR story rehearsed until it sounds fluent, the list of talking points.
Then a question arrives in a form they never rehearsed, and the list runs out.

Answer Engineering replaces the list. It treats answering as an engineering discipline: a question is
an input, the interviewer's hiring decision is the output, and there is a process that reliably gets
from one to the other. The process is internal, transferable, and it works on questions you have never
seen. You will not memorize answers. You will learn why excellent answers are excellent, so you can
construct one on demand.

## The Argument in the Name

Engineering is the discipline of building reliably to a spec, under constraints, and proving it works.
That is exactly what a strong interview answer requires. The spec is the signal the interviewer is
collecting. The constraint is two minutes and your nerves. The proof is specificity no weak candidate
could fake. Most interview prep treats the answer as a performance to rehearse; this book treats it as
an artifact to engineer. Same word, same rigor, applied to the thing that gets you hired.

## You Interview on the Portfolio

This book does not exist in a vacuum. The artifacts you built in the rest of the library are the
resume: the coding agent, the fleet, the eval engine, the exam. They are not exercises to discard once
the course ends; they are the evidence you walk an interviewer through. A reader mid-way through the
other books can start here today; a reader who finished them already has the artifacts and can treat
this as the final unlock. The building and the proving compound.

## What You Build

The book runs in three phases. First the framework: an internal four-step Algorithm for any interview
question, behavioral, technical, or systems-design. Then the example banks, where you watch the
Algorithm applied across the question types an AI Engineer faces until the moves become automatic.
Then the portfolio layer, where you turn your own artifacts into interview-ready narratives.

Your portfolio artifact here is not code; it is a **prep dossier**, the career analogue of a project
repo. Every lesson ends in a Claude Code exercise that adds to it: decomposition logs, full answer
constructions, self-scored against a rubric, deliberate-practice records. By the end you can prove your
readiness the same way the exam book proves a system: against a checker, not a feeling.

You already did the hard part. You built something real. Now you learn to make a stranger see it in
the time it takes to answer one question.

Turn the page. Module 1 starts where every answer starts: with the question, and the instinct you have
to unlearn.
