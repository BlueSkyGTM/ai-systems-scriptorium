# Sequencing the Preparation

Preparation is not infinite. The interview loop grades you on whether the right things are ready at
the right stages, not on how much time you spent, so the only question that matters is sequence: what
do you build first, what has the most leverage, and what do you cut when the clock is short?

The dossier you have built across this book answers that question. Each piece maps onto one or more
stages of the loop, and the stages are not equal. The structure of the dossier tells you the order.

## The Dossier as a Map

Six artifacts, each mapping to one or more stages of the five-stage loop:

- **The decomposition log** records your breakdown of AI Engineer job descriptions: the skills named,
  the signals behind them, the patterns across listings. It is the foundation the rest of the
  dossier rests on, not a stage artifact by itself.
- **The answers log** holds your full Algorithm runs on technical and behavioral questions. It feeds
  fluency. Every stage requires you to explain something clearly; the answers log is where you have
  practiced doing that.
- **The behavioral bank** is your set of structured STAR entries for ownership, influence, conflict,
  and failure categories. It directly prepares the hiring-manager round and the panel, which is two
  of the five stages.
- **The systems-design log** holds your design runs: open-ended prompts you worked through under a
  clock, with production reasoning over a real architecture. It prepares one stage: the
  systems-design round.
- **The portfolio-narrative document** is your written account of your best artifact, the decisions
  behind it, the tradeoffs you made, and where it falls short. It prepares the recruiter screen, the
  portfolio deep-dive, and the panel, three of the five stages.
- **The deliberate-practice reps** in your calibration log feed `calibrate.py`, which scores your
  readiness category by category. Categories have to reach READY before certain stages can be graded
  as ready. The reps are not a stage artifact; they are what make the other artifacts worth trusting.

If you have run this book in order, you have all six. If you are coming in late, the order they
appear on that list is not the order to build them.

## Where the Leverage Is

The dossier's leverage is unequal, and the sequencing guidance tells you exactly where it sits.

**Build the framework and the answers log first.** They feed everything. The Algorithm is how you
think on your feet in every round; the answers log is how you have practiced that thinking. Without
them, the rest of the dossier is decoration.

**Build the behavioral bank next.** It covers two stages: the hiring-manager round and the panel.
That is the highest stage coverage of any single artifact, which makes it the highest-leverage thing
you can build after the foundation is in place. Both stages require depth in specific categories:
the hiring-manager round needs ownership and influence; the panel needs conflict and failure. Those
four categories are the minimum viable behavioral bank.

**Build the portfolio-narrative document third.** It prepares three stages: the recruiter screen,
the portfolio deep-dive, and the panel. One document, written once and well, earns a return in three
different rooms. That is the best ratio of authoring effort to stage coverage in the dossier. The
depth of the document matters: a shallow overview will not survive the portfolio deep-dive, where
the interviewer will pull on your decisions and failure modes. Write for the deep-dive, and the
recruiter screen gets covered as a side effect.

**The systems-design log is last, but not because it is least important.** The systems-design round
is the single hardest stage to prepare, and it covers only one stage. That combination means it
demands the most reps for the narrowest payoff; it is high cost and non-optional. Budget the most
practice time to it, but build the behavioral bank and the portfolio-narrative document first so the
high-cost artifact does not crowd out the high-leverage ones.

**One stage is not prepared by the dossier at all.** The technical coding screen, covered in Module 4,
is sourced separately. The dossier does not grade it and this module does not pretend otherwise.
Name it in your loop plan as a stage you are sourcing outside this book, and do not let the gap
surprise you.

The explicit priority order, from highest to lowest:

1. Framework and answers log (foundation; feed all stages)
2. Behavioral bank (highest stage coverage; two stages)
3. Portfolio-narrative document (second-highest coverage; three stages, one document)
4. Systems-design log (single stage, highest difficulty; plan the most reps)
5. Deliberate-practice reps (run `calibrate.py`; drive categories to READY before you submit a stage as ready)

## The Time-Boxed Plan

The dossier does not care how you feel about your readiness. `check_prep.py` checks completeness;
`calibrate.py` checks quality. Both produce a verdict, not a feeling. Your preparation plan should
work the same way: set a time constraint and cut by leverage, not by comfort.

**With two weeks:** run the full priority order. Build the foundation in the first two days. Spend
three days on the behavioral bank: four categories, real STAR entries, scored against the rubric.
Spend two days drafting the portfolio-narrative document and one more revising it against a
deep-dive prompt. Use the remaining time for systems-design reps, aiming for at least five logged
runs in your systems-design log. Reserve the final two days to run `calibrate.py`, close any
category that is not READY, and fill in `loop-plan.md` for every stage.

**With two days:** cut everything that does not directly cover a stage and cut the lowest-coverage
artifact last. Build the foundation in the first two hours. Write the portfolio-narrative document,
since it covers three stages and can be drafted in a few hours. Draft the behavioral bank entries
for the two categories each stage needs at minimum: ownership and influence for the
hiring-manager round, conflict and failure for the panel. Do one systems-design run and log it.
Run `check_prep.py` to see what is missing. What `check_prep.py` flags as absent, you cut; do not
invent entries to pass the check.

The check, not the feeling, is the exit condition.

## Core Concepts

- The behavioral bank has the highest stage leverage of any single dossier artifact, covering two of
  the five interview stages.
- The portfolio-narrative document covers three stages (recruiter screen, portfolio deep-dive,
  panel); one strong document earns that return three times.
- The systems-design log prepares the single most difficult stage and demands the most practice reps,
  even though it covers only one stage.
- When time is short, cut by stage coverage: build the framework and answers log first, then the
  behavioral bank, then the portfolio-narrative document; the systems-design log follows even if it
  gets fewer reps than it deserves.
