# Module 6: The Sans Python Portfolio as Resume

Phase 2 prepared you to reason to a strong answer when you are put on the spot. This phase is about the
thing you bring into the room with you: the work you already built. If you have worked through Sans
Python, you have a portfolio of runnable systems, a terminal coding agent, a production RAG chatbot, a
governed multi-agent fleet. Those are not exercises to leave behind. They are the resume. This module
teaches you to walk an interviewer through one of them in a way that makes them trust your judgment.

The mistake most engineers make with a portfolio is treating the walkthrough as a code reading. They
open the file and narrate what each function does. The interviewer did not ask what the code does; they
can read code. They asked what you built and why, and the gap between those two answers is where offers
are won. A walkthrough is the reasoning behind the artifact, told to someone who was not there when you
made the decisions.

## What the Interviewer Is Actually Evaluating

When an interviewer says "walk me through a project," they are running a hypothesis: can this person make
me understand a system I have never seen, quickly, and then show me the judgment that went into it? They
are listening for the decisions you made, the alternatives you rejected, the tradeoffs you accepted, and
the failure modes you designed against. A walkthrough that lists components scores low. A walkthrough that
defends choices scores high. It is the same discipline as justifying every box in a systems-design round,
except now the system is real and you are the one who shipped it.

## How to Read a Worked Walkthrough

Every worked walkthrough in this module runs the same motion: the sixty-second overview that orients the
listener, then the decision tour that goes deep on the three or four choices that mattered. Read each one
for the reasoning being foregrounded, not for the artifact's source code. The failure mode of portfolio
prep is a memorized monologue, the same speech delivered to every interviewer regardless of the role or
the follow-up. The walkthroughs here are demonstrations of how to construct the narration from the
artifact and the role in front of you, so you can do it for your own work and adapt when the interviewer
steers.

## The Four Lessons

1. **The Artifact Walkthrough** builds the shape: the sixty-second overview, then the decision tour, and
   the discipline of what to foreground versus what to skip.
2. **Foreground the Decisions** goes to the decision layer at depth: every component was a choice, and the
   interviewer is buying the why, not the code.
3. **Tailoring to the Role** narrates the same artifact differently for an applied-AI role, a platform
   role, and a research-adjacent role, because the walkthrough bends to the signal the role values.
4. **The Portfolio Narrative Document** composes the written walkthrough you bring to interviews, then
   turns the weak-walkthrough audit on it the way earlier modules audited an answer and a design.

## What This Module Feeds Forward

By the end you have a portfolio-narrative document in your prep dossier: a structured, interview-ready
walkthrough of at least one artifact, with the decisions, tradeoffs, failure modes, and role-tailoring
written down and self-audited. That document is what you rehearse from before a portfolio round, and the
last two modules build on it directly: live practice puts it under pressure, and the hiring-loop module
composes it with everything else in the dossier into the full preparation you walk in with.
