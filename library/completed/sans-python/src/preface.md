# Preface

Most courses teach you everything and prepare you for nothing.

This one is built the other way around. It teaches a single job; Production AI Engineer, the person who
builds, serves, and governs the systems that run models; and it cuts everything that doesn't serve that
job. What's left is dense on purpose.

You will notice the writing is short. That is not a shortcut; it is the method. This book follows William
Zinsser's *On Writing Well* the way an engineer follows a spec. Clutter is a defect. Every sentence is
stripped to its cleanest components, every qualifier that earns nothing is cut, every lesson holds one idea
and one voice. If a page reads fast, that is the work, not the absence of it. You learn faster when nobody
is wasting your attention.

The book holds the concepts. You build everything else. Each lesson ends with a button that hands its work
to Claude Code, open beside this page in your editor; the lesson tells you what to build and where you left
off, and you build it. Reading happens here; the doing happens there. The two are one loop.

You will also notice what is missing. There is no chapter on training a model from scratch, no deep dive on
diffusion, no survey of frontier-safety policy. That is deliberate. Those things are real and worth knowing
they exist. They are not on the path to this job, so they are not on the path of this book.

How the work compounds: you build single agents, you compose those agents into teams, and the team builds
the final system. Nothing is busywork. Every artifact solves a real problem and earns a place in a
portfolio.

Read it in order. The sequence is the argument.
