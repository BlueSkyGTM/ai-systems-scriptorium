# Module 2: The Algorithm in Detail

You can run the loop now. Module 1 installed the four steps and you have walked them on clean questions:
decompose, identify the signal, construct, stress-test. That is the difference between having a method
and having none. It is not yet the difference between a candidate who passes and one who gets the offer.

This module is the depth. The four steps stay the same; what changes is that you run them the way someone
runs them after fifty interviews, not after one.

## What Changes Here

Module 1 taught the steps on questions that arrive clean: one clear primary signal, time to think, a
single answer to construct. Real loops are not clean. The primary signal is ambiguous or shifts when the
interviewer follows up. The room reacts and you have to re-read it live. The answer has to come out of
your mouth in two minutes without losing the thread, and sometimes you realize halfway through that you
aimed wrong. And the difference between a senior answer and a staff answer is a bar you have to hold
yourself to, because no one in the room will hold it for you.

Each lesson here takes one step of the Algorithm and goes past where Module 1 stopped. It assumes you
know the basics cold; it does not re-teach them. It teaches the internal monologue: what an expert is
actually thinking at each step, and the hard cases that separate fluent from rehearsed.

## The Four Lessons

1. **The Question Behind the Question** deepens decomposition: the latent signal beneath the stated one,
   the follow-up that reveals the real probe, and how the primary hypothesis shifts when the same
   question is asked of a senior versus a staff candidate.
2. **Reading the Room** makes signal identification dynamic. The interviewer's reactions are data; you
   recalibrate the hypothesis to the company, the role, and the level as the conversation moves.
3. **Thinking Out Loud** is construction under live pressure: the internal monologue made audible, the
   two-minute and thirty-second versions of one answer, and how to recover when you have drifted.
4. **The Weak-Answer Audit** turns the stress test into a system: the full catalog of answers that kill
   offers, run as a self-audit, against the senior-versus-staff bar.

## What This Module Feeds Forward

Module 1 made the Algorithm a method you can follow. Module 2 makes it a reflex you can trust under
pressure. The example banks that follow, behavioral, technical, and systems-design, assume you run all
four steps live without effort; this module is where that stops being effort.
